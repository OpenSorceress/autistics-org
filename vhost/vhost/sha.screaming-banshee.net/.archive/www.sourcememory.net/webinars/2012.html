Treasures of African Women, II

The greatness they denied, but you always knew existed. Classic Nok and other ceramics of ancient Nigeria, stone sculptures of Esie and the magnificent Benin bronzes. Yoruba, Hausa and Ashanti queens; priestesses and women's rites; ancestral mothers of Mali, Congo and Tanzania. Female artists in velvet, leather, mudcloth, weaving, calabashes, and muralists from Burkina Faso to Botswana. Women builders, lyre-players, rain-shrine oracles -- and modern women who resist unjust laws, patriarchal violence, and stand up for their rights and their most ancient cultural traditions.
