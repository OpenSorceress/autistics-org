Child Does Not or Cannot Give Consent

When the child is unable to give consent, or refuses in any form to take the meds --

This goes back to the basic right that anyone (regardless, again, of age or disability) has, to choose not to alter their own brain. Putting meds into someone's system, against their will, with the intent of changing their neurochemistry, is one of the most terrible and invasive forms of violation. It ranks up there with involuntary neurosurgery (i.e. lobotomies, etc).

This also means, that if the person taking the meds decides against the meds at any point, they must be given a way to safely go off of the meds (it usually involves slow tapering, to avoid withdrawal symptoms).
