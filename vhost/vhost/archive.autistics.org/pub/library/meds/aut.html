Autism, Asperger Syndrome, PDD, PDD-NOS

When I use "Autism" "Asperger's Syndrome" "PDD" and "PDD-NOS" in reasons that are not adequate for giving meds, it is because those are truly not adequate reasons. Behavior that is commonly associated with those things (self-stimulatory behavior, talking out of turn, not paying attention to language or people, sensory differences, social oddness or withdrawal, etc) is not truly pathological (there's nothing really wrong with it in and of itself) and is not an acceptable reason to give medication. Medication for "making a kid look more normal" is one of the least acceptable and most frightening for those of us who are autistic. Beware of any doctor who says "Oh, they're autistic? I know medications that work for that" or claims that a medication is "for autism".
