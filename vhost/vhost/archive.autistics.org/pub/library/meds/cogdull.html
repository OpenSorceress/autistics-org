Antipsychotics and Cognitive Dulling

I have read many parents who seem unsure about the "cognitive dulling" part of antipsychotics. Sometimes the dulling is obvious, and shows in the autistic's behavior, test performance, or academic performance. At other times, the dulling manifests more subtly, and may not be noticeable even by the autistic.

When on medications which did not make their cognitive effects immediately noticable, I interpreted my tendency to be "duller" at certain times, as just a natural part of my personality or brain chemistry. The dullness was not always enough to be felt, but it was there. Much of it only became apparent once I began to reduce the dosage, and finally eliminate, my antipsychotic medication, and suddenly my mind felt extremely clear and focussed. I only recognized it in direct contrast to being on medications. If someone had asked me while on the antipsychotic (and people did), I would have said I felt totally normal.

I note that many drunk driving accidents occur because the drunk person "feels totally normal" and safe to drive. Obviously, it is not safe for them to drive. This also applies to people on antipsychotic medications. I also note that the medications which were more likely to cause this "dulling without feeling it" effect were the Atypical Antipsychotics, which are falsely reputed to be "safer".

I have been off of an "atypical" antipsychotic for almost a year. I am still trying to recover all of my cognitive functions, which are coming back very slowly.
