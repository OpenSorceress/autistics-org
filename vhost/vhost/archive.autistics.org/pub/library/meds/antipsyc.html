Antipsychotic Medications and Autism

Antipsychotics should not be given to autistics.

(Quoted from above): Antipsychotics dull cognitive processes. When a child is developing, they need all the cognitive processes they can get. When a child is autistic and developing, they really need all the cognitive processes they can get, to cope with the world and grow mentally. Antipsychotics also have many possible side-effects that are truly horrible, and can even cause hallucinations and wildly non-standard reactions in autistics.

  • More about Cognitive Dulling
  • "Atypical" or "Safer" Antipsychotics (and why they're not so safe)
  • Non-Standard Reactions in Autistics

The professionals who prescribe antipsychotics have little to no understanding of what they are doing. They just say they do, because they are "experts". If they knew what they were doing, they wouldn't do it. Nothing is worth what antipsychotics do to a person, either short-term or long-term.

I have nearly died because of severe non-standard reactions to antipsychotics. In addition, the signs of my reaction (most noteably, difficulty breathing, but also including swollen tongue and inability to open my mouth widely) were seen as "noncompliant behavior" and ignored until it was almost too late. I do not say it lightly when I say that giving an autistic an antipsychotic is playing Russian Roulette with their life.
