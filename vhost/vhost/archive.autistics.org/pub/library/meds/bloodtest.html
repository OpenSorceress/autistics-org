Autism Information Library

Blood Level Testing

The person must have access to testing of levels of the medication in the bloodstream. This is important, though not always seen as such, because a toxic reaction to a medication is a truly horrible thing. Autistic people are particularly likely to be sensitive to medications, so it is more of a concern.

While on a psychiatric medication, I was not tested for blood level for a long time. I developed seizures and severe constipation, both of which were ignored and treated as "noncompliance" and "behavior problems". After almost a year of being on it, my blood level was tested. I was at at least twice the "very toxic" level. The medication was promptly discontinued.
